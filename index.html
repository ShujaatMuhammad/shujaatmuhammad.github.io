About

Research with biological meaning.

Building reproducible computational approaches that link predictive accuracy to interpretable genomic insight.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Shenzhen University working at the interface of bioinformatics, deep learning, and regulatory genomics. My work centers on computational models that identify and characterize promoter sequences across bacteria, phages, plants, yeast, and archaea.

Across doctoral and postdoctoral research, I have developed machine-learning and deep-learning tools for promoter prediction, classification, and genome annotation. I combine robust model design with sequence representation, motif analysis, and explainable AI to make outputs more meaningful for biological interpretation.

My current interests include protein language models, generative models for enzyme discovery, and transformer-based representations for biological sequence analysis.

“My goal is not only to predict a label, but to reveal the sequence patterns that make the prediction biologically useful.”

Research

Core research directions

Method development across genomic regulation, protein sequences, and data-driven biological discovery.

01 · REGULATORY GENOMICS

Promoter prediction and classification

CNNs, hybrid networks, and tailored sequence encodings for promoter detection in bacterial, phage, archaeal, plant, and yeast genomes.

02 · TRUSTWORTHY AI

Explainability for sequence models

SHAP, motif extraction, and biological validation that connect neural predictions with recognizable regulatory sequence features.

03 · PROTEIN AI

Protein language models

Representation learning with ESM and ProtBERT for function prediction, protein interaction studies, and enzyme-focused discovery workflows.

04 · GENERATIVE BIOLOGY

Designing novel sequences

Generative pipelines based on VAEs and diffusion methods for candidate enzyme and regulatory-sequence generation.

05 · MEDICAL AI

Image-based clinical analysis

Deep-learning systems for retinal fundus analysis and clinically motivated detection of diabetic-retinopathy-related exudates.

06 · REPRODUCIBLE SYSTEMS

Accessible research software

Web servers, practical codebases, and transparent computational pipelines that allow broader use and scrutiny of methods.
